## Sleep Monitoring System

### Cradlewise Smart Crib {#cradlewise-crib}

**The crib.** Replaces a separate bassinet, crib, video monitor, white noise machine, and mattress. Birth to 24 months — converts from bassinet (elevated) to full crib as baby grows.

**Why we picked it:**
- Self-soothing is the best feature. AI detects when baby starts stirring — before they cry — and gently bounces to soothe them back to sleep. White noise kicks in too. Saved us countless trips to the nursery.
- One purchase replaces ~$2,000+ worth of separate nursery gear.
- Sam Altman recommended it — that's how we found it.

**What it does:**
- Converts from bassinet to crib — starts elevated so you're not bending down, lowers as baby grows
- Learns baby's sleep patterns over time and adapts
- Tracks sleep through the app — duration, wake-ups, patterns
- Sends alerts to your phone when baby is crying, stirring, or waking
- Built-in HD camera with night vision, audio monitoring, room temperature sensor
- White noise, pink noise, brown noise + Spotify
- Organic mattress, mattress protector, and two fitted sheets included
- We signed up for the annual Nurture subscription ($200/yr) for full app features

**Setup tips:**
- Took us 45 minutes to assemble — do it in the room where baby will sleep.
- Book at least 1 week before your due date. They schedule delivery up to a week after your selected date including shipping. Don't cut it close.

**Cost breakdown:**
- Crib: $1,999
- Tax (~8%): ~$160
- Annual subscription: $200
- Protection plan: $120 (we're moving soon, worth it)
- **Total first year: ~$2,479**

[Cradlewise](https://cradlewise.com/product/cradlewise-smart-crib/v7/)

### Owlet Dream Sock {#owlet-dream-sock}

**Tracks baby's pulse rate and oxygen while they sleep.** The peace of mind that lets you actually close your eyes. Useful from 1–18 months (6–30 lbs).

**Why we picked it:**
- FDA-cleared — first and only baby monitor with that clearance. Same pulse oximetry tech hospitals use.
- Base station glows green when readings are normal. Flashes red and alarms if something leaves preset zones.
- Very accurate in our experience. Works fine alongside the Cradlewise bouncing.

**What it does:**
- Soft sock wraps around baby's foot
- Tracks live pulse rate, oxygen levels, sleep trends
- 16-hour battery life
- Comes with 4 fabric socks (2 sizes, left and right)
- HSA/FSA eligible

**Practical notes:**
- **Placement matters.** Follow the guide — wrong position = false readings.
- **Range is limited.** Walk too far from the base station and the alarm goes off.
- **Wi-Fi setup:** Only connects on 2.4 GHz. If you have Eero or similar mesh, temporarily disable 5 GHz to pair. Eero lets you turn it off for 30 minutes.

**Cost:** ~$354 (sock + tax + protection plan). Owlet360 subscription adds deeper analytics and daily reports.

[Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Owlet-Dream-Sock-Indicators-Movement/dp/B09NCGQSY9?th=1) · [Owlet](https://owletcare.com/products/owlet-dream-sock) · [Babylist](https://www.babylist.com/gp/owlet-dream-sock-smart-wearable-baby-monitor/24519/1064827)

## Bath Kit System

*We strongly recommend placing the tub in the shower and using it there. Easier to control the water, faster to fill and drain. Much more practical than bending over a bathtub.*

> ⚠️ **On bathing frequency:** Follow your pediatrician's guidance. At our 2-month appointment, the doctor told us V.G.'s dry skin was from bathing too often. Every 3rd or 4th day is enough. On the days in between, if baby is smelly, wash with water only — no soap. Alternate between soap and no-soap washes. Autoimmune and skin sensitivity issues are real. Don't over-bathe.

## Orbit Modular System

*The Orbit system is built around a single SmartHub — the same mount in the car base docks onto the stroller frame. No adapters, no compatibility headaches.*

**Why Orbit Baby:**
- **No leaning into the car.** Base rotates 360°. Turn the seat toward the door, strap baby in standing upright, rotate back to rear-facing. Same in reverse when taking baby out.
- **Side impact protection.** Deep bucket shape fully lined with EPP foam — the most protection we found in infant car seats.
- **One hub, everything connects.** Same SmartHub in the car docks onto the stroller frame. No adapters.
- **Build quality.** Founded by two Stanford engineers in 2004. Premium price, but the materials back it up.
- **JPMA certified.** Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association — independent safety testing beyond minimum federal requirements.

**Why we bought separately (not a bundle):** Orbit sells bundles with a bassinet ($1,700–$2,050). The bassinet is **not certified for overnight sleep** — Orbit's own site says it's "only intended for supervised sleep." If you don't need it, the bundle price doesn't add up.

**Why we bought direct from Orbit:** Car seats are hard to return at most stores. Orbit offers **60-day free returns with free return shipping** and a **2-year warranty** — more generous than Nordstrom or specialty retailers.

**What else we considered:** Nuna Pipa Urbn was the runner-up — lighter (~7 lbs vs ~14 lbs), baseless install, well-built. But shorter height range (29" vs Orbit's 32"), no modular system, and Orbit folds compact and stands on end.
