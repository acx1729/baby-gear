### Instant-Read Food Thermometer

**Check milk temperature before every feed.** Aim for 99–105°F.

- The bottle surface is always warmer than the milk inside — touching the outside tells you nothing.
- Milk warmers don't measure actual milk temp. This does.
- Place in milk after warming, read in seconds. Also useful later for baby food.

**Cost:** ~$10
